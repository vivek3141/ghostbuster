I don't have a heart. She tapped her chest and a hatch opened, exposing whirring gears and a compartment. But if I did, it would belong to you. Reaching in, she pulled something out. It was a small, delicate, porcelain figurine of a ballerina, gracefully posed. As she handed it to him, her eyelashes fluttered with vulnerability. "Here," she whispered, her voice like chimes in the wind. "This is my heart. It may not beat, but it holds all the love I have for you." He stared at the figurine, feeling a rush of emotion. In that moment, he knew her heart was more real than any flesh-and-blood organ.
