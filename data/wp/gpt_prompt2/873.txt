As our car barreled down the desolate highway, tension hung thick in the air. Panic lurked in our eyes as we gazed at the ominous mushroom cloud on the horizon. Silence descended upon us. My heart pounding, I turned to my family, desperately forming the words that might be my last. "I never told you," I began, my voice trembling, "but I ate the last slice of pizza yesterday." My family's panicked expressions morphed into a mixture of confusion and annoyance. Dad's grip tightened on the steering wheel; Mom shot me a disappointed glare. The looming threat of impending doom now overshadowed by my trivial confession. A painfully awkward car ride ensued.
