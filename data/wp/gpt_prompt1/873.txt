As the ominous looming of destruction cast a dark shadow over our road trip, we braced ourselves for the worst. With trembling voices, I gathered my loved ones close, preparing for our impending end. "I just want you all to know," I stammered, my heart pounding, "I used the last of the milk this morning." Silence engulfed the car as everyone exchanged confused glances. For the remainder of the journey, an awkward tension filled the air, a bizarre reminder of our futile anticipation. In that moment, we realized that even in the face of catastrophe, trivial matters could still be uncomfortably significant.
