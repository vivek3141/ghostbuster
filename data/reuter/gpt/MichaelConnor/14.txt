Sunbeam Corp. has announced that it is drastically cutting down its workforce and product lines in an effort to turn the struggling company around. The decision, which is expected to impact half of Sunbeam's employees and 87 percent of its products, is being made to help the company focus on its core business.
The announcement was made by Sunbeam's CEO, John Jackson, in an internal memo sent to the company's employees on Monday. In the memo, Jackson explained that the company had been facing significant challenges in recent years due to competition and supply chain disruptions.
"Despite our best efforts, we have not been able to keep up with the changing market dynamics and have been losing ground to our competitors," Jackson wrote. "To survive and thrive, we need to make some tough decisions and take bold actions."
Jackson went on to explain that the company would be reducing its product lines to just 13, focusing on the most profitable and high-growth areas of the business. The remaining products will include kitchen appliances, bedding, and outdoor living products.
The decision to cut half of the company's workforce was not taken lightly, according to Jackson. He acknowledged that it would be a difficult time for those impacted by the layoffs and committed to providing support to those affected.
"We understand that this is a difficult and challenging time for our employees, and we are committed to providing them with the necessary resources and support during this transition," he wrote.

Despite these challenges, the company has managed to generate revenue of $1.2 billion last year, up two percent from the previous year. However, profits have been under pressure, and the company has been forced to cut costs and restructure its business.
