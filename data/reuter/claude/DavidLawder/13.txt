Lear Corp. to Acquire Instrument Panel and Trim Parts Unit of Borealis Holding AB to Expand Total Automotive Interior Systems 

Lear Corporation, a leading Tier 1 supplier to the global automotive industry, today announced it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Forward Automotive Technologies, a subsidiary of Borealis Holding AB that specializes in instrument panel and trim parts. The acquisition will strengthen Lear’s global automotive interior systems capabilities and enable new integrated solutions for customers.

Forward Automotive Technologies is a trusted supplier of injection-molded instrument panel components, decorative trim parts, and other interior products to major automakers including Volvo, BMW, and Mercedes-Benz. The company operates four manufacturing facilities in Germany, China, Mexico, and South Africa and generates approximately $450 million in annual revenue.  

“The acquisition of Forward Automotive Technologies will enhance Lear’s product capabilities in instrument panels, trim components and other interior parts, allowing us to provide more comprehensive solutions to our customers,” said Ray Scott, Lear President and CEO. “We have identified substantial synergies and new growth opportunities from combining Forward Automotive Technologies with our existing European and North American interior systems operations.”

The all-cash transaction for $525 million is expected to close in the third quarter of this year, subject to regulatory approvals and other customary closing conditions. Lear expects the acquisition to be neutral to earnings per share in 2020 and accretive to earnings in 2021 and 2022.

Lear Corporation supplies automotive seating and electrical systems to automakers around the globe. With nearly 169,000 employees in 39 countries, Lear is a Fortune 500 company that ranks #147 in the Fortune 500 with global headquarters in Southfield, Michigan, a suburb of Detroit. 

The acquisition continues Lear's strategy to strengthen its product portfolio and global footprint through investments in technologies, components, and materials that enhance the customer experience, reduce environmental impact, and facilitate advances in connectivity and automated driving.
