Lear Corp. to Acquire Borealis Industrier AB to Complete Total Automotive Interior Systems

Lear Corporation (NYSE: LEA), a leading global supplier of automotive seating and electrical systems, today announced it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Borealis Industrier AB, a Swedish company that designs and manufactures innovative thermoplastic elastomer technologies for the automotive industry. The acquisition of Borealis will strengthen Lear’s global position as a leading supplier of advanced automotive interior systems by adding unique capabilities in specialized elastomer technologies.

“The addition of Borealis is a key step in Lear’s strategy to provide our customers with a complete set of advanced interior system solutions,” said Ray Scott, Lear's President and CEO. “Borealis’s innovative thermoplastic elastomer technologies and expertise in engineered polyolefin plastics are an excellent strategic fit for us. By combining our respective portfolios, we will be able to offer enhanced integrated seat systems with unparalleled comfort, durability, and functionality.”

Under the terms of the agreement, Lear will acquire all outstanding shares of Borealis for $875 million in an all-cash transaction. The transaction is expected to close in the third quarter of 2018, subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals.

Headquartered in Piteå, Sweden, Borealis currently operates eight production facilities across Europe and Asia with approximately 2,200 employees. The company had calendar year 2017 sales of $620 million. Borealis’s unique and proprietary polypropylene-based thermoplastic elastomers provide elasticity, softness, and UV resistance for automotive applications like seating, door panel, console substrates and gaskets.

The transaction will be funded through a combination of cash on hand and Lear’s existing revolving credit facility. Lear expects the transaction to be accretive to its earnings in 2019.
