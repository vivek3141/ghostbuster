South Africa played a significant role as a transport hub for the Allies during World War II, particularly concerning the racial dynamics and treatment of troops of color. Smith's methodology involves analyzing historical sources and narratives that highlight the experiences of Allied troops in South Africa. This is a historical paper with a focus on race and hospitality. The author relies on firsthand accounts, personal narratives, and historical records to provide evidence for the argument. The main point of the article is to shed light on the often overlooked contributions and experiences of troops of color in South Africa during the war. The author's goal is to challenge prevailing narratives that downplay or ignore the racial dynamics in South Africa's role in World War II. The article exposes systemic prejudices and discriminatory treatment faced by troops of color. By highlighting these narratives, Smith aims to provide a more comprehensive understanding of South Africa's involvement in the war.
