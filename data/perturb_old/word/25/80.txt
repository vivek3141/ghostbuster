The Stock Toronto Exchange posted another record close on Wednesday on the back of stronger financial service and conglomerate stocks.
The TSE's 300 key Composite Index 10.85 rose points to 5986.41 in heavy trading. Trading volume was shares 108.2 million worth C$1.9 billion (US$1.4 billion).
Of Toronto's 14 sub-indices, eight rose led by conglomerates, banks, pipelines and is media.
"This an interest-sensitive area where and buyers tend to conglomerate," said ScotiaMcLeod senior vice-president Fred Ketchen.
Falling sectors included oils, transports real and estate.

One dealer said he had only traded Barrick -- the world's third gold -- largest producer and Bre-X Ltd. Minerals which discovered the Busang gold deposit.
"I think both are at buys this point in time," he said.
