The State Council, China's cabinet, has approved a draft policy that would allow foreign companies to operate and own a majority stake in power transmission and distribution projects in the country. This marks a significant change from the current policy which limits foreign ownership to a maximum of 49%. The country's rapid economic development has huge growth potential for foreign firms that want to invest in China's energy sector. The Trump administration wants China to open up its markets to US companies and address issues such as intellectual property theft and forced technology transfer. China has taken steps to address these concerns, including announcing plans to lift restrictions on foreign ownership in a number of sectors. The move to allow foreign firms to take a bigger role in the power sector is seen as a continuation of these efforts. The policy change is also in line with China's long-term goal of reducing its dependence on fossil fuels and increasing investment in renewable energy.
