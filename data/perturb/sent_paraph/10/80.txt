Financial service and conglomerate stocks helped the Toronto Stock Exchange post another record close on Wednesday. In heavy trading, the TSE's key 300 index rose 10.85 points to 5986.41. There was a trading volume of over 100 million shares. Of Toronto's 14 sub-indices, eight rose led by conglomerates, banks, pipelines and media. "This is an interest-sensitive area and where buyers tend to conglomerate," said ScotiaMcLeod senior vice-president Fred Ketchen. The sectors that fell were oils, transports and real estate. Barrick Gold Corp. led Toronto's active issues after news on Tuesday of a deal which will give it a majority share in a rich Indonesian gold deposit. One dealer said he had only traded Barrick -- the world's third largest gold producer -- and Bre-X Minerals Ltd. which discovered the Busang gold deposit. "I think both are buys at this point in time," he said. As Canada's Big Six banks began reporting year-end earnings this week, bank stocks were snapped up by investors. Bank of Nova Scotia made its first annual profit on Wednesday. On the broader market, declining stocks edged out advances 556 to 437, despite gains in the key index. The shares were not changed. The U.S. markets will be closed on Thursday. Barrick Gold rose 0.75 to 39.65 on more than 7.1 million shares, while Bre-X Minerals gained 0.65 to 21 on 6.5 million shares. Bank of Nova Scotia had 745,000 shares. Toronto-Dominion Bank jumped 0.80 to 36.85 in heavy turnover of 1.3 million shares. T-D Bank is scheduled to report its fourth quarter results on Thursday. * Newbridge Networks Corp. jumped 1 to 38.60 on 1.5 million shares. The stock regained some lost ground since analysts' downgraded Newbridge last week.
