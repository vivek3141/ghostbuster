President Franklin Roosevelt’s acts and laws played a crucial role in ending the Great Depression and significantly improving the situation for certain marginalized groups of the population, including immigrants, African Americans, and workers. Through his New Deal programs and policies, Roosevelt brought about social and economic change that helped restore the nation’s stability and spurred progress.
This act ensured fair treatment of immigrants and promoted equality.
Roosevelt's policies helped African Americans who were disproportionately affected by economic disparity.
Workers, who were at the epicenter of the economic crisis, witnessed significant improvements due to Roosevelt’s intervention. He passed the National Industrial Recovery Act (NIRA), which guaranteed workers the right to join unions and engage in collective bargaining. The Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) introduced minimum wage laws and set limits on working hours, consequently improving the quality of life for workers across the nation.
The nation's recovery from the Great Depression was impacted by President Franklin Roosevelt's acts and laws.
