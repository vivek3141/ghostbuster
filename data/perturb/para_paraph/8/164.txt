Children's television shows and movies are extensions of capitalist values according to Adorno.
The amount of merchandise associated with popular children's franchises is an example of media products reinforcing capitalist values.
Contrasting Adorno's perspective, structural functionalism suggests that media products for children serve a more positive function in society. According to this approach, these products fulfill children's developmental and educational needs, providing them with entertaining and informative content. For instance, educational shows like "Sesame Street" have been praised for their ability to foster learning and social development among children. Moreover, the media also serves to unify society by providing cultural narratives and shared experiences.
The positive social contributions of media products are emphasized by structural functionalism.
According to Adorno, media products for children reinforce capitalist values.
