The rise and fall of the Enron Corporation was nothing short of a financial disaster, leaving an indelible mark on the corporate world. Enron, once considered a paragon of the energy industry, collapsed under the weight of its inherent corporate greed and fraudulent practices. This essay will examine the circumstances and factors that shaped Enron's struggle, evaluate its fatal mistakes, and analyze theories and assumptions about its bankruptcy.
The culture of arrogance and excessive risk-taking emerged at the heart of the corporation under the leadership of Jeffrey Skilling and Kenneth Lay.
The corporation created numerous off-balance-sheet entities known as Special Purpose Entities.
The company also engaged in market manipulation, specifically in the California electricity market during the energy crisis of 2000
The board of directors failed to hold executives accountable for their actions, which led to the company's downfall.
The agency theory suggests that executives prioritize their own interests over that of shareholders, leading to a misalignment of incentives.
Corporate greed, fraudulent practices, and a lack of oversight and accountability led to the downfall of the company.
