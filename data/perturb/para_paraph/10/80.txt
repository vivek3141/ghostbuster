Financial service and conglomerate stocks helped the Toronto Stock Exchange post another record close on Wednesday.
The TSE's key 300 index rose 10.85 points to 5986.41 in heavy trading.
Of Toronto's 14 sub-indices, eight rose led by conglomerates, banks, pipelines and media.
ScotiaMcLeod senior vice-president Fred Ketchen said that buyers tend to conglomerate in this area.
Falling sectors included oils, transports and real estate.
After news of a deal which will give it a majority share in a rich Indonesian gold deposit, Toronto's active issues were led by Barrick Gold Corp.
The dealer said he only traded Bre-X Minerals, which discovered the Busang gold deposit.
"I think both are buys at this point in time," he said.
As Canada's Big Six banks began reporting year-end earnings this week, bank stocks were snapped up by investors.
Bank of Nova Scotia made its first annual profit on Wednesday.
On the broader market, declining stocks edged out advances 556 to 437, despite gains in the key index. Another 294 shares were unchanged.
The U.S. markets will be closed on Thursday.
