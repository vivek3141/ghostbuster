The main theme of the epic is the quest for immortality.
The message of The Epic of Gilgamesh is that humans must accept their mortality and that immortality is impossible.
The human desire to challenge the gods' authority is shown in the message.
The Mesopotamians acknowledged the gods' powers and their place in the order in the story.
The significance of this message extends beyond the realm of mythology. The Epic of Gilgamesh serves as a cautionary tale, reminding humanity of the futility of pursuing immortality. It emphasizes the importance of cherishing our limited time on Earth and finding meaning in our mortal existence. In today's world, where the pursuit of material wealth and fame often overshadows the value of human life, The Epic of Gilgamesh serves as a timeless reminder of our shared mortality.
The theme of immortality and the message that humans must accept their finite existence are explored in the epic.
